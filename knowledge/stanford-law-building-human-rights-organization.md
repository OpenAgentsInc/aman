# Building a Human Rights Organization to Last
Source: https://law.stanford.edu/2020/01/23/behind-the-scenes-building-a-human-rights-organization-to-last/
Category: Organizational design and operations
Organization: Stanford Law School (Mills Legal Clinic)

## Overview
An article describing lessons from the Guernica Group's annual meeting and five takeaways for building sustainable human rights organizations.

## Key takeaways
- Define a theory of change and keep scope aligned.
- Choose a structure fit for purpose, including hybrid models.
- Build one team and one vision across distributed staff.
- Use strategic communications based on target audiences.
- Professionalize processes to protect staff and operations.

## Example uses
- Draft a scope and theory-of-change memo for a new NGO.
- Evaluate whether a nonprofit, hybrid, or network model fits the mission.
- Create SOPs for travel, reimbursements, and field safety.

## Notes
- The article emphasizes operational work as the foundation for impact.
